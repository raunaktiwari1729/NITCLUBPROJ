Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about clubs at NIT Kurukshetra.

Start with 1–2 clubs that genuinely excite you. Quality beats quantity — being an active member of one club is far more rewarding (and looks better on your resume) than being a passive member of five. You can always explore more in your second year once you've settled in.
Yes, absolutely. Most clubs at NIT KKR have no binding commitment. If a club doesn't match your expectations, you're free to step back at any time. Use the Club Compass reviews and forum to research before joining so you make the right choice from the start.
No, clubs are entirely optional. However, they are one of the best ways to build practical skills, make friends outside your branch, and strengthen your profile for placements and higher studies. The college experience is significantly richer with club involvement.
Time management is key. Most clubs hold meetings 1–2 times a week for 1–2 hours. Avoid over-committing during exam season — good clubs will understand. Use the events calendar on Club Compass to plan ahead and never be caught off guard by clashing deadlines.
Most clubs recruit at the start of each semester — typically in July–August and January–February. Some technical clubs hold open recruitment year-round. Check each club's profile on Club Compass for their current recruitment status (Open / Closed / Coming Soon).
Not necessarily! Many technical clubs like coding and robotics actively welcome beginners and teach skills from scratch. What they look for is enthusiasm and willingness to learn. Read the club reviews on Club Compass to understand what each club actually expects from freshers.
The Club Compass quiz is a 10-question personality and interest assessment. Based on your answers, our algorithm ranks clubs best suited to your profile and suggests a primary domain. It takes under 2 minutes and you don't need to be logged in to take it — though logging in saves your results.
Club Compass is a student-built platform made by NIT KKR students, for NIT KKR students. It is not an official institute portal. All reviews and opinions are from real students and do not represent the official views of NIT Kurukshetra or any of its clubs.
